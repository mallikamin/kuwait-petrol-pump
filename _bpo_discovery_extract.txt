Walk us through your EXACT bifurcation process:

   Example: "Total PMG sold = 1000 liters. Manager sits at computer, sees total is 28,000 KWD.
   Manager enters: Cash = 20,000 KWD, Credit = 5,000 KWD, Card = 3,000 KWD. System validates
   total matches. Manager saves."

  Your process:
    � Total sales recorded: PMG � 1,000 liters, HSD � 1,000 liters.
    � Initially, petrol pump operators are unable to identify how much cash received relates to PMG or
         HSD, as this requires additional tracking resources.
    � The accountant first reviews all credit sale invoices (petrol pump slips) and records them in the
         POS system with proper classification between PMG and HSD.
    � Next, sales made through bank cards and pump cards are entered into the system.
    � Once all identifiable transactions have been recorded, the remaining balance is treated as cash
         sales and allocated accordingly.
